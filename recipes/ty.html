Tang Yuan

Description

Tang Yuan is a delightful Chinese dessert made of glutinous rice flour filled with sweet or savory fillings and served in a warm, fragrant syrup. These colorful and chewy rice dumplings symbolize togetherness and are commonly enjoyed during festivals and special occasions in China.

Ingredients

  • Glutinous rice flour
  • Water
  • Filling (can be sweet or savory, such as red bean paste, sesame paste, or minced meat)
  • Syrup (made with sugar and water)

Steps

  1. Mix glutinous rice flour with water to form a dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small portions and flatten them into circles.
  3. Add your chosen filling to the center of each dough circle.
  4. Seal the dumplings, rolling them into smooth balls.
  5. Boil the Tang Yuan until they float to the surface.
  6. Prepare the syrup by simmering sugar and water.
  7. Serve the Tang Yuan in the warm syrup, creating a delicious and comforting dessert. Enjoy!
